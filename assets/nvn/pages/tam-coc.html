Tam Coc

The waterway through the mountains

Photo by Chiến Nguyễn Bá (accessed on Pixabay.com)

What to do

  • Paddle along the Ngo Dong River - This is why people come to Tam Coc, two hours of epic scenery in a small boat staring at mountains and fields of yellow rice. Unforgettable.
  • Visit a pagoda - This area has many, but there are two particular highlights; Bich Dong that is carved into a cliff-face and Bai Dinh which is the largest pagoda in Southeast Asia!
  • Stop and listen to the birds - Thung Nham Bird Park is a reserve home to a number of species of rare birds. For a small fee you can ride out through the wetlands on a small boat and feel lost in nature itself for a little while.

What to eat and drink

  • Fine dining - Grilled Goat Meat (De Nuong) is a highlight of all mountainous areas in the North, but in Tam Coc you will find the best. Charcoal grilled, Naturally-fed and marinated with lemon and coconut.
  • From the street - Crispy Burnt Rice Cakes (Com Chay) is a common snack on the streets of Tam Coc. Rice is cooked, mixed with meat and seasoning, shaped into a cake, then fried in oil a second time until golden.
  • For the adventurous - Mountain Snails (Oc Luoc Xa), either eaten boiled with hotsauce or steamed with ginger and lemongrass are an uncommon delicacy that everyone should enjoy.
