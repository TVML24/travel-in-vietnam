Ha Long

The bay of Limestone towers

What to do

  • Get out on a boat - There are hundreds of vessels to choose from; including modern large yachts and refitted traditional flat bottomed fishing vessels. The ideal way to see this beautiful place!
  • Hit the kayaks - There are multiple platforms set up throughout the bay where you can rent a kayak or canoe and experience the caves and bays from a bit closer to the water!
  • See the floating villages - There are a few floating villages scattered throughout the bay, home to fisherman that found it more convenient to build a bigger boat than to return to the land after a hard days work. A fascinating experience.

What to eat and drink

  • Fine dining - Oysters (Hau Nuong) are a delicacy in many parts of the world, but experience them here fresh caught, seasoned with salt, pepper and lemongrass before being grilled over charcoal.
  • From the street - Squid Cake (Cha Muc) is made throughout the wider province, but the best is to be found in Ha Long, where it is freshest. Ground and mixed with seasoning before being grilled a golden brown.
  • For the adventurous - Sea Worms (Sa Sung) are an expensive delicacy and are only available in Ha Long Bay as they do not live in unclean water. Try them stir-fried with garlic, mustard and chilli.
