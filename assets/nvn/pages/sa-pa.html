Sa Pa

The village in the misty mountains

Photo by Trinh Xuan Dao (accessed at Pixabay.com)

What to do

  • Climb Mount Fansipan - The tallest mountain in Vietnam and only a stones throw away from the main township. For the adventurous there are three sclimbs with varying difficulty, for those only looking for a photo there is also a cablecar.
  • See the park on Ham Rong - For those wanting a more mystical experience, try climbing Ham Rong and experiencing the cultivated gardens, ancient statues and local stalls at the peak.
  • Visit Cat Cat Village - One of the oldest in the region and intentionally left as close to it's original design as possible, Cat Cat showcases how the local H'Mong people live amongst the ancient mountains.

What to eat and drink

  • Fine dining - Seven Colour Sticky Rice (Xoi Bay Mau) is a great choice for a sweet-tooth. This dish comes from the Nung people and is coloured with juice from local plants in the region.
  • From the street - Bamboo Sticky Rice (Com Lam) is sticky rice wrapped in a banana leaf, stuffed into a bamboo tube and chargrilled with pork or chicken. Very convenient when on the move.
  • For the adventurous - Horse Soup (Thang Co) is a horse based dish that seeks to not waste any of the animal including it's intestines! Simmered for hours with spices and local fruit.
