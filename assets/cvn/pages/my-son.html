My Son

Ancient Temple of a Forgotten Civilisation

What to do

  • Visit the My Son museum on site - The museum houses some of the more ancient and vulnerable artifacts and will give you a good overview of the historical and cultural significance of the site before you get started.
  • Get a tour guide - Personally I live doing things on my own, but in My Son, having an expert with you that can describe what the ruins were and tell you the story of each building definitely makes a difference!
  • Hunt for evidence of the war - Soldiers from both sides hid in the region at various points of the war and various bomb craters and scarring can still be seen, don't stroll too far off the path though, there are still live munitions around!

What to eat and drink

  • Fine dining - Sliced Barbacue Veal (Cau Mong)- to the North of My Son and on the way if you are travelling from Da Nang, in Dien Ban District you can find this local speciality of long-cooked rotissary veal.
  • From the street - Tapioca Dumplings (Banh Bot Loc) - to the South of My Son in Tam Ky this special street food can be found. Sweet-ish on their own and wonderfully savoury when dipped in fish sauce, these dumplings are delicious.
  • For the adventurous - Mountain Chicken (Ga Quay) - South again from My Son is the district of Que Son. Here you can enjoy the unique dish of mountain chicken. Simply select which chicken you want and the staff will kill it and turn it into a variety of distinct dishes for you to enjoy. You will eat ALL of the chicken, nothing is wasted!
