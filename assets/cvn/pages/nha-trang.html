Nha Trang

Gateway to the Sea

What to do

  • Visit Thap Ba Hotsprings - The perfect spot to relax for an afternoon, with your choice of heated or cool pools. There is even a mudbath on offer for the adventurous that claims to have excellent effects on the bather's skin.
  • Browse through the National Oceanographic Museum - As understandable for a country with such a long coastline, there is a fascination with the sea here and you can experience a variety of ocean creatures first-hand here! A favourite with kids!
  • Get out on a boat! - Small boats are easy to hire in Nha Trang and it doesn't cost much to get a trip around the local islands by sailboat. An experience you are unlikely to forget.

What to eat and drink

  • Fine dining - The local lobster (Ho Tom Hum Cang) comes from nearby Lobster Island (Binh Ba) and in Nha Trang you will find that it is especially fresh and delicious.
  • From the street - Jellyfish noodles (Bun Cha Ca Sua) - Rice noodles served in a thick broth with fried fish cakes and jellyfish that provides a delightful crunchy texture to this sublime dish.
  • For the adventurous - White Sardine Salad (Goi Ca Mai) - A beautful salad with whole baby sardines mixed in along with herbs, starfruit, green banana and chilli. Not your typical salad, but worth a try if you liek something different.
