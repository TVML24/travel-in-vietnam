Da Lat

The land of eternal spring!

What to do

  • Walk through Da Lat Flower Gardens - These manicured gardens planted over seven square kilometers and containing more than three hundred unique species of flowers are a beautiful place to stop a relax for a little.
  • Take a locomotive from Da Lat Train Station - Stop by at the oldest operating train station in Vietnam and get on a wood-burning locomotive for a short trip up a mountain pass to a traditional village at it's peak.
  • Visit the summer palace of the last emporer - The last Emperor of Vietnam Bao Di spent summers in Da Lat and his home can still be seen today with all of the original furniture and personal belongings of the final monarch.

What to eat and drink

  • Fine dining - Terracotta Pot Noodles (Bun Tho) are a favourite noodle dish for the colder weather of the Da Lat region. With a spiced broth made from Pork and coconut and a variety of delectable seafood ingredients cooked alongside the local noodles, this is a true gourmet take on rice noodles.
  • From the street - Meatball soup with fresh bread (Banh Mi Xiu Mai) is a classic breakfast throughout Da Lat. This dish consists of Vietnamese meatballs served in a thick chicken broth alongside fresh baguette. Can you think of anything nicer for a cold winter's morning?
  • For the adventurous - Rice cakes and chicken offal (Banh Uot Long Ga), involves boiling the meat and organs in a ginger broth and serving on a bed of wet gelatinous rice cake. Another warming Da Lat offering if a little different!
