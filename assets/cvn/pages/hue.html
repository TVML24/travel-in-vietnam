Hue

The Royal Capital of the last Dynasty

What to do

  • Visit the tombs of the long dead emperors - the final resting place of Tu Duc, Minh Mang and Khai Dinh can all be found in Hue and all are worth your time. Stunning exmaples of restored traditional architecture and ancient traditions.
  • Marvel at the Hue Citadel - The Center of Imperial Government, a fortress laid out in the French style complete with cannon, Royal gardens, water features and museums.
  • Experience the Royal Theatre - Watch a traditional Vietnamese performance of dance and music in what was once the heart of the Palace Culture. There are also galleries and exhibitions for those who want to see even more.

What to eat and drink

  • Fine dining - Beef Noodles Hue-style (Bun Bo Hue) although this dish can be found all over Vietnam and of various quality, it's worth it to splash some cash in Hue to get the original experience with prime cuts of beef.
  • From the street - Happy Cake (Banh Khoai) is a street food found all over Vietnam, but is expecially known for it's quality in Hue. A yellow fried crepe folded over dried meats, sprouts and sometimes glass noodles. Fried again until extra crispy.
  • For the adventurous - Clam Rice (Com Hen) is stir-fried clams mixed into fried rice and served with mixed vegetables, peanuts and herbs. This may not sound as adventurous as some foods, but it's reputation for turning stomachs is well known.
